ABOUT US / OUR STORY

Why are we doing this?

72% of nonprofits faced disruption during COVID-19. The idea of The Supply Chain Project was born out of a passion for improving how these nonprofits impact vulnerable populations. When for-profit supply chains fail, people are inconvenienced, when non-profit supply chains fail—people die.

Corporate partnerships can help.

  • Our founding team has worked in both the corporate and non-profit arenas. We believe it is important for non-profits and corporations to collaborate and leverage their collective resources to create partnerships for positive change.
  • This type of collaboration creates sustainable supply chain networks that strengthen the bonds between local industries and at-risk communities so businesses can make a real impact on social issues that benefit all levels of society.

Delivering on a promise.

Nonprofits spend 25% of their budget on logistics

  • From the outset, our focus has been on delivering tangible results for nonprofits in need. We work closely with logistics providers and technology providers to deliver on the needs and build sustainable supply chain solutions that meet those needs. Whether it's getting critical aid to disaster-stricken areas, improving access to essential medicines, or reducing food waste, we strive to make a real difference in the world.

The Hidden Opportunity

Every organization holds unused value in many forms.

From inventory to expertise, billions in resources sit idle—unseen, unconnected, and underutilized.

LACK OF VISIBILITY

Resources are fragmented across systems, spreadsheets, and silos—making it impossible to see the full picture.

INABILITY TO RESPOND

Disconnected processes, manual workflows, and limited coordination prevent timely action and effective matching.

LOST OPPORTUNITY

The result is higher costs, wasted resources, missed impact, and unmet needs for communities.

FORMS OF UNUSED VALUE

Across every operation, resources go unused every day.

INVENTORY

All available or stranded inventory—raw materials, finished goods, components, and surplus.

CAPACITY

Facilities, equipment, storage, real estate, and operational assets—utilized or not.

TRANSPORTATION

Empty miles, unused fleet capacity, access constraints, and underutilized routes.

LABOR

Skills, volunteer capacity, and time availability across teams and communities.

KNOWLEDGE

Expertise, intellectual capital, and insights that are invisible, siloed, or underutilized.

TIME

Underutilized time, scheduling gaps, and timing mismatches.

FUNDING

Unallocated dollars, grants, credit capacity, and financial resources.

TECHNOLOGY

Systems, data, tools, and digital capabilities not fully leveraged.

THE CORE PROBLEM

Lack of visibility and inability to respond prevent resources from reaching the people who need them.
