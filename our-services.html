What kind of services are available?

For Nonprofits, you can request logistical support (transport, storage, and related), training, and advisory in optimization projects.

For Companies, you can request advisory for donations entry and tax declarations. You can request support for your CSR projects and employee engagement in volunteer projects.

Tier 1: Reshape Demand

We begin with the needs of communities.

Nonprofits function as differently configured retail or distribution operations—focused on serving people, not profit.

COMMUNITY NEEDS

  • Understand real needs on the ground.
  • Include who is affected, what is needed, and why.

RECEIVING, STORAGE, & FULFILLMENT CAPACITY

  • Assess ability to receive, store, and fulfill goods.
  • Consider space, equipment, people, and processes.
  • Ensure safe, efficient, and equitable distribution.

OPERATIONAL PREPAREDNESS

  • Evaluate operational readiness across people, processes, and systems.
  • Plan for surge, continuity, and reliability.

LOCAL DEMAND PLANNING

  • Define assortment and service discipline.
  • Set categories, trade lanes, seasonality, and priorities.
  • Avoid "send us anything" models.

We start by listening—so we can deliver what's needed, not what's left.

Right resources. Right place. Right time.

Tier 2: Match + Mobilize

We orchestrate the right match and mobilize resources.

TSCP connects available resources with vetted nonprofits and ecosystem partners to meet real needs—not by owning assets, but by mobilizing our network to deliver impact.

AVAILABLE RESOURCES

  • AVAILABLE GOODS & INVENTORY — Identify and prepare excess goods, materials, and products for a purposeful second life.
  • STORAGE & FACILITY CAPACITY — Leverage warehouse space, storage, and facilities to receive, stage, and distribute.
  • TRANSPORTATION CAPACITY — Mobilize fleet capacity, routes, and transportation assets to move resources efficiently.
  • EXPERTISE — Engage knowledge and skills to solve complex operational and community challenges.
  • TECHNOLOGY PARTNERS — Apply platforms and tools for visibility, matching, coordination, and reporting.

PARTNERS & EXECUTION POINTS

  • VETTED NONPROFITS — Trusted nonprofit partners who understand local needs and deliver to the communities they serve.
  • LOGISTICS PARTNERS — 3PLs, warehouse operators, and fulfillment providers that enhance capacity and efficiency.
  • TRANSPORTATION PARTNERS — Carriers, fleet operators, and mobility networks that expand reach and reduce cost.
  • STRATEGIC MARKETPLACE PARTNERS — Retailers, manufacturers, and suppliers who provide product, funding, or in-kind support.
  • OUTCOMES — Drive measurable results that improve lives, strengthen communities, and protect the planet.

WE MOBILIZE, NOT OWN

TSCP does not own every asset or function. We mobilize our network to deliver impact at scale.

The Assessment Framework

A comprehensive view to uncover value and ensure the right match.

1SUPPLY-SOURCE ASSESSMENT

  • Supplier network reliability
  • Source location & distance
  • Delivery frequency & lead time
  • Sustainability & compliance

2GOODS & RESOURCE ASSESSMENT

  • Type, quantity, quality, and condition
  • Shelf life / expiration & seasonality
  • Packaging, labeling, and compatibility
  • Value & replacement cost

3TRANSPORTATION ASSESSMENT

  • Route options & transport modes
  • Capacity, frequency, and reliability
  • Access constraints & infrastructure
  • Cost per mile & emissions

4CAPACITY & PREPAREDNESS ASSESSMENT

  • Storage & facility capacity
  • People, skills, and operational readiness
  • Systems, processes, and governance
  • Risk, safety, and continuity

5LOCAL DEMAND & ASSORTMENT ASSESSMENT

  • Community priorities & needs
  • Assortment & service discipline
  • Trade lanes, seasonality & timing
  • Equity, access, and geographic fit

6IMPACT ASSESSMENT

  • Lives & communities impacted
  • Resource utilization improvement
  • Cost avoidance & efficiency
  • Environmental & social outcomes
