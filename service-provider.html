Partnering for Impact

GET INVOLVED / SERVICE PROVIDERS

Let's make a difference.

Join us in connecting professionals and providers with distribution-intense non-profits.

Nonprofits must distribute food, medical equipment and supplies, clothing, shelter, and general wellness products, which require adept distribution capabilities. The Supply Chain Project focuses exclusively on enabling nonprofits to close the gap and leverage similar solutions that successful for-profit enterprises enjoy.

What kind of services can you provide non-profits?

  • Logistics provider
  • Technology provider
  • Professional consultant
  • Service provider

Provider Categories

Logistics Providers

Non-profits are always in need of ways to transport, store, or distribute goods. Become one of our partners that can help non-profits achieve that. We are looking for:

  • • Local-level transportation
  • • National-level transportation
  • • Regional-level transportation
  • • International-level transportation

Technology Providers

The non-profits we connect with are distribution intense. They are in a constant need of technology to manage their growing impact. Here are the top tech providers we want to work with:

  • • Fulfillment platforms
  • • Logistics management solutions
  • • Dispatch software
  • • Data Analytics providers

Service Providers

Non-profits have a wide range of needs in a changing environment. Administrative and operation-level services provided by small, medium, and large businesses are sought after on our Marketplace.

Industry Professionals

Persons with expertise can help non-profits access knowledge, good practices, and technology so organizations can execute their projects more effectively. Access to consulting in:

  • • Supply chain management
  • • Project management
  • • Logistics planning
  • • Financial consulting
  • • Legal advice

Our focus is to take the best from the for-profit supply chain world and marry it with the best of the nonprofit arena to create solutions that evens the playing field for Nonprofits. Today no NGO is too small or too big to benefit from an improved supply chain.

CEO and Founder

Improving and streamlining supply chain activities is critical to non-profit organizations. Simplifying work, streamlining operations, and professionalizing services allow non-profits to spend more of their scarce resources on their mission.
